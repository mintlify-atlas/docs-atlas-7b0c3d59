## Why Quantization Matters

As language models grow larger, running them becomes increasingly expensive and resource-intensive. Quantization addresses this by reducing the precision of model weights and activations, allowing you to:

- **Reduce memory footprint** by 2-8x or more
- **Speed up inference** on consumer hardware
- **Lower deployment costs** significantly
- **Run larger models** on devices with limited resources

<Note>
  Modern quantization techniques can reduce models to 4-bit or even lower precision while maintaining most of their performance.
</Note>

## Key Concepts Explored

### Post-Training Quantization (PTQ)

Learn how to quantize already-trained models without additional training. PTQ is the most practical approach for most use cases:

- Convert existing model weights to lower precision
- Minimal impact on model quality when done correctly
- Fast and easy to apply
- Works well with calibration datasets

### Quantization-Aware Training (QAT)

Understand how to train models with quantization in mind:

- Simulate quantization during training
- Model learns to be robust to reduced precision
- Better performance than PTQ at very low bit-widths
- Higher training cost but better final results

### INT8 Quantization

The guide provides detailed visualizations of how 32-bit floating-point weights are converted to 8-bit integers:

- Mapping floating-point ranges to integer ranges
- Scale and zero-point calculations
- Maintaining numerical accuracy
- Hardware acceleration benefits

<Info>
  INT8 quantization typically reduces model size by 4x while maintaining 99%+ of the original model's performance.
</Info>

### BitNet: Extreme Quantization

Explore cutting-edge research in extreme quantization:

- **Ternary parameters**: Reducing weights to just 3 possible values (-1, 0, +1)
- Massive compression ratios
- Specialized hardware opportunities
- The future of efficient model deployment

## Quantization in Practice

The guide illustrates practical considerations:

- Which layers to quantize (and which to keep in full precision)
- Calibration dataset selection
- Evaluating quantized model quality
- Hardware-specific optimizations

### Mixed-Precision Strategies

Not all model components need the same precision:

- Keep attention weights in higher precision
- Quantize large feedforward layers more aggressively
- Balance memory savings with quality degradation
- Layer-specific quantization strategies
